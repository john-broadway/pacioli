# The plates — sources and licenses

Every image in this directory is public domain or CC0. Verified against Wikimedia
Commons `extmetadata` on 2026-07-17; source URLs are the exact files fetched.

## tavola-1-pacioli-portrait.jpg

- **Portrait of Luca Pacioli** (with a young man, probably Guidobaldo da Montefeltro),
  attributed to Jacopo de' Barbari, 1495. Museo di Capodimonte, Naples.
- The friar at his slate (marked EVCLIDES), the glass rhombicuboctahedron hanging at
  left, a dodecahedron resting on the Summa at right.
- Source: `https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/2/2a/Pacioli.jpg` (1500×1250)
- License: **Public domain** — the painter died before 1516; faithful photographic
  reproduction of a public-domain work.

## tavola-2-leonardo-polyhedron.jpg

- **Vigintisex Basium Planum Vacuum** — the hollow rhombicuboctahedron, drawn by
  Leonardo da Vinci for Luca Pacioli's *De divina proportione* (published Venice 1509).
- The same solid that hangs in the portrait; Leonardo illustrated Pacioli's book.
- Source: `https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/1/12/De_divina_proportione_-_Vigintisex_Basium_Planum_Vacuum.jpg` (1235×1338)
- License: **Public domain** (Commons `LicenseShortName: Public domain`).

## summa-spread.jpg

- **Summa de arithmetica, geometria, proportioni et proportionalita**, Luca Pacioli,
  printed by Paganino Paganini, Venice, 1494. An opening from the Metropolitan
  Museum of Art's copy — *Ragione de Partitori*, worked arithmetic in columns.
- Source: `https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/a/a0/Summa_de_Arithmetica_MET_DP-12871-001.jpg` (4000×3144)
- License: **CC0** (The Met's Open Access public-domain dedication).
